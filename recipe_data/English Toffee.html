English Toffee Recipe

makes roughly (1) 13×9 pan

1 cup granulated sugar
1 cup unsalted butter
2 tsp water
6 oz. semi-sweet chocolate chips
1  tsp vanilla extract
a generous pinch of salt

about 1/2 cup (60 g) finely chopped almonds OR
Coarse sea salt for topping

A WORD OF CAUTION: Boiling sugar is hot. Like “molten lava” hot. be respectful and careful of it, and have a bowl of ice water handy in case you need to dunk your hand in it immediately.

Prepare a 13 x 9 cookie sheet with either a silicone baking mat or parchment paper. If you use parchment paper, dab a little butter in the corners of the pans to keep it in place; you don’t want to be wrangling parchment paper with 300 degree toffee on it.

Melt the butter, sugar, salt and water together in a saucepan over low heat. Make sure that the saucepan can hold at least double the amount you put into it; when we get this boiling it is going to bubble up and expand and you definitely don’t want boiling sugar all over you (see above). Attach a candy thermometer so that it sits in the liquid, but does not touch the bottom of the pan.

While the butter is melting, stir as little as possible, and as gently as possible. Stir just enough to make sure nothing is burning on the bottom. Once the butter has melted, turn the heat up to medium.

Here you can see the toffee has begun to boil. It is approaching the soft ball stage. Once the toffee starts to boil, stir constantly.

Here the toffee is approaching hard ball stage. You can see the volume has really puffed up as all the water in the butter boils off.

This toffee is just about ready to come off of the heat. It has reached hard crack stage at about 310 degrees farenheit, has collapsed down in volume from where it was in the hard ball stage. The color should resemble peanut butter; if it doesn’t, leave it on for a minute and keep stirring. The color develops VERY quickly, so you need to concentrate and never stray from the pot when you’re cooking sugar. Remove from heat and stir in vanilla extract quickly.

As soon as the vanilla is stirred in, pour toffee onto the prepared 13  x9 pan, and using a silicone spatula, spread it into a thin layer. Don’t worry about thin spots or uneven edges, once we chop it up, they won’t be noticeable.

Let set for 1 minute. Sprinkle chocolate chips over the toffee, and using a silicone spatula, spread the chocolate chips out over the toffee.

Coat the toffee with your favorite toppings! You can use anything you want- Almonds like you see here, crushed peanuts or hazelnuts, crushed up peppermint sticks, or one of my favorites- a couple pinches of coarse sea salt.  The salt brings out flavors of the chocolate that you don’t get without it.  I tend to use better chocolate when I’m going to use sea salt.

Chill in the fridge until the chocolate is set. break into bite size pieces and chop if you’re going to use in ice cream.

This is about how big I like my chunks for ice cream

Enjoy!
