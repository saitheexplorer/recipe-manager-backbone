Ingredients:

2 (cooked) cups white rice

2 heads bok choy

1-2 cloves garlic to taste

1-2 tablespoons ginger to taste

1-2 teaspoons sesame oil

Soy sauce

(optional) sesame seeds

Set a pan/wok to high heat, add oil. Sautee the ginger and garlic first, then add the bok choy. Stir fry for 3-4 minutes until the hearts of the bok choy start to split when pressed against firmly with a spatula. Season with salt/pepper/soy sauce to taste. Pour the bok choy into a bowl to rest.

Add 2-3 more tablespoons of oil to the pan, and if you're adding sesame seeds to the rice, put them in for a while before adding the rice. Season with soy sauce to taste and stir fry for 2-3 minutes. Turn off the heat(!!) and add the sesame oil. Stir to combine and serve with the bok choy.

IMPORTANT: Do not use sesame oil for your stir-fry! It burns too easily and leaves a bitter taste in your mouth. Sesame oil should only be used to add a slight aroma, not for an actual "sesame" taste. Peanut and canola oil have high smoke points, so use those to actually stir fry anything. Olive oil is also an ok option, but just be wary that extra virgin burns quite easily as well.
