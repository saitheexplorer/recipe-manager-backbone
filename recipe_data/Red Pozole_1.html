Prep Time: about 2 hours

Serves:Â 10 people

Ingredients:

  • 2.2 pounds White Hominy (precooked, if possible).
  • 2.2 pounds Mushrooms (champignons)
  • 10 Guajillo Peppers
  • 5 Bay Leaves
  • 1/4 cup Olive Oil
  • 2 Garlic Cloves
  • 1 tablespoon Oregano
  • 2 Onions
  • 1 tablespoon Thyme
  • Lots of Water
  • Salt and Pepper to taste

Directions

  • Rinse 2.2 pounds of champignons and chop them in long pieces.
  • Finely chop 1/2 onion and 1 garlic clove.
  • Heat 1/4 cup of olive oil in a pan over medium heat.
  • Toss into the pan the mushrooms, onion, garlic and add salt to taste. Fry and brown all the ingredients for about 10 minutes or until soften; stir constantly.
  • Once the ingredients are ready transfer them to a bowl.
  • Toss 2.2 pounds of precooked white hominy and 1 onion in a large stock pot and cover with water.
  • Bring the white hominy to a boil, then reduce to low heat and add 1 tablespoon of oregano, 5 bay leaves, 1 tablespoon of thyme, salt and pepper to taste.
  • Stir well all the ingredients, cover the stockpot and simmer for 1 hour.

Pozole Salsa:

  • Remove the veins and seeds of 10 guajillo peppers. What we are looking for is the smokey flavor not the hotness.
  • Put the peppers in a saucepan, cover with water, bring them to a boil and keep the water boiling for 5 minutes.
  • Transfer the peppers to a blender and pour 1/4 cup of broth, take it from the stockpot where the hominy is being cooked. Also add 1 garlic clove, 1/2 onion, 1 teaspoon of oregano, salt and pepper to taste.
  • Blend until pureed.

1 hour later...

  • You will notice that the hominy is bigger, softer and a bit broken. This means that the hominy is ready.
  • Toss the mushrooms (the ones that were set aside in the bowl), pour the salsa strained and stir to combine all the ingredients.
  • Cover the pozole and simmer for 15 minutes.
  • Verify is the pozole needs salt. If you want discard the bay leaves and the onion that was added at the beginning.
